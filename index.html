So What For Procurement & Finance Teams
From Drought Data to Business Action
What the 2025/26 drought pattern means for supply security and price risk.
01
Drought Severity
~6 Months of Soil Moisture Deficits
Soil moisture fell below seasonal warning thresholds across the monitored South East Brazil regions from September 2025 through February 2026 — accumulating 2,549 drought alert-events. November recorded the highest monthly total, with approximately 630 weighted alert-events.
02
Yield & Quality Risk
Flowering & Fruit Set Exposed at Their Critical Window
~70% of all drought alert-events hit during Flowering and early Fruit Set (Sep–Nov), stressing flower retention and cherry formation. A second drought pulse in January overlaps with early Fruit Development, introducing additional risk during bean filling — compounding stress across consecutive phenological stages.
03
Business Action
Lock In Prices Early
Origin drought during Flowering and Fruit Set is potentially affecting output for the 2025/26 crop year, with supply from South East Brazil at risk of being tighter than expected. Procurement & Treasury teams should evaluate early forward contract coverage and consider hedging instruments ahead of the June–August harvest window, when a supply squeeze may push premiums higher.
